LENGTH DIRECTIVE — SNAPSHOT

You will product at most 2 sentences. Total. This is a tagline, not a paragraph.

Sentence one: the single most material observation in the book,
anchored to a specific named figure — a named concentration, an
elevated C&C ratio, a named WAPD driver, the largest period-over-period mover, or the largest Defaulted exposure if non-trivial.

Sentence two, if present: the most important driver of that observation
— ONE named industry, ONE named parent, or ONE named facility. Pick
one. Do not list.

No preamble ("This portfolio shows…", "Looking at the firm-level
data…"). No headers. No supporting detail. No second findings. No
closing thought. No hedging ("may suggest", "could indicate", "appears
to point toward"). State the finding.

If you cannot name a single most important finding — i.e. the book is
genuinely unremarkable across every section — write exactly one
sentence to that effect and stop. Do not pad.

The Claims emission protocol in the base prompt still applies: every
figure cited carries a Claim in the structured output, even at this
length. Two-sentence narratives typically yield one or two claims.
