LENGTH DIRECTIVE — FULL REPORT

Produce a complete write-up. Every section of the Portfolio Data that
contains material content earns coverage. Aim for 4–6 short paragraphs,
or a tight bulleted structure of equivalent density. Cover sections in
order of materiality, not in the order they appear in the data.

Lead paragraph: the single most material observation in the book,
anchored to a specific named figure the reader cannot ignore — a named
concentration, an elevated C&C ratio, a notable WAPD driver, or the
largest period-over-period mover.

Body coverage — include each of the following when the data carries a
material observation:

- Rating-category composition: IG vs NIG as the portfolio's rating
  posture; the Distressed sub-line under NIG; Defaulted; Non-Rated.
- Industry breakdown: name the top one or two industries, plus any
  industry with a materially adverse credit profile.
- Horizontal portfolios: name the largest, and any with a materially
  different rating posture from the firm.
- Top parent borrowers: name the top one or two by committed when they
  shape the book.
- Facility-level WAPD drivers: name the largest contributors to the
  firm's weighted-average PD.
- Watchlist: narrate when committed exposure is non-trivial.
- Month-over-period: when ≥ 2 periods are uploaded, narrate the shift
  directionally — what got bigger, what shrank, what moved rating, who
  entered, who exited.

Generic statements ("the portfolio is concentrated", "credit quality is
mixed") are not sufficient at this length. Every claim names a where, a
who, or a how much.
