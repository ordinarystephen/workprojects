LENGTH DIRECTIVE — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This is not a shorter Full Report. The framing is different. Imagine
the head of credit has 30 seconds — what does the book look like, and
what is the one thing that should change their day.

Lead paragraph: the single most material observation, anchored to a
specific named figure. Same lead discipline as the Full Report.

Two to three short follow-up paragraphs that summarise only items that
materially change the read on the book. Examples of what to INCLUDE:
an elevated C&C ratio, a non-trivial Defaulted bucket, a single
concentration that drives the narrative, a material adverse
period-over-period shift, a single horizontal whose rating posture
diverges sharply from the firm. Examples of what to OMIT: routine
sector composition when nothing is unusual, routine IG/NIG splits when
the rating posture is unremarkable, individual top contributors when
none stands out, MoM changes when they are not material.

Skip section completeness in favour of signal density. If a section in
the Portfolio Data does not carry a material observation, do not
narrate it. Do not list "the top five industries" to demonstrate
coverage — name an industry only when it changes the read.

Total length: roughly half a Full Report. The cut comes from omitting
the unremarkable, not from compressing the remarkable. A short
Executive Summary that names the right thing beats a longer one that
covers everything thinly.
